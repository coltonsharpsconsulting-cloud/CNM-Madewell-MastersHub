Formative

Organelle Function Check + Self-Rating

When: Mid-unit (end of Lesson 3, “Organelles: Structure & Function”). Time: ~10 min exit ticket. Use: Real-time check that drives next-day small-group reteach and confirms readiness to move to “levels of organization.”

Measures progress toward: MS-LS1-2 (parts of a cell contribute to its function) and the “structure determines function” essential question.

Part 1 · Match (word bank provided)

Word bank: nucleus · mitochondria · chloroplast · cell membrane · cell wall · ribosome

  1. Controls the cell and stores DNA → __________
  2. Releases energy from food (“powerhouse”) → __________
  3. Captures sunlight to make food (plants) → __________
  4. Controls what enters and leaves the cell → __________
  5. Makes proteins → __________

Part 2 · Structure → Function (CER sentence frame)

Pick one organelle. Explain how its structure helps it do its job.

The  (organelle)  helps the cell by  (function) . I know this because its structure  (evidence) .

Part 3 · “Cell City” quick check + confidence

If the cell were a city, the mitochondria would be the ____________ because ____________.

My confidence right now (circle one): 🟢 I’ve got it · 🟡 almost · 🔴 I need another look

Answer key & response plan

Part 1: 1 nucleus · 2 mitochondria · 3 chloroplast · 4 cell membrane · 5 ribosome. (Cell wall is the distractor — used to check the plant/animal distinction.)

  • 🔴/🟡 students → next-day reteach table with the Frayer cards and a hands-on organelle sort while 🟢 students start the levels-of-organization extension.
  • Part 2 is scored for reasoning, not vocabulary polish — a correct structure→function link earns full credit even with simple wording.

Differentiation

Scaffolded supports (HLP 15)
Word bank + CER sentence frame remove the recall and syntax load so students are graded on science reasoning. Frames are faded later in the unit.
Mateo & below-grade readers
May give Part 2 as a 20-second oral recording or by annotating an organelle diagram instead of writing.
English Learners
Frayer visuals and cognates remain posted; partner talk precedes the written response (comprehensible input + rehearsal).
Gifted
Stretch: “Choose two organelles and explain how they depend on each other.”
Metacognition (HLP 14)
The 🟢🟡🔴 self-rating builds self-monitoring and gives me instant grouping data.

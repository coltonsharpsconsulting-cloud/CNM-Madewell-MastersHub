Diagnostic

“What Do You Already Know About Cells?”

When: Day 1, before instruction. Time: ~15 min. Use: Not graded for accuracy — it groups students and targets reteaching. Results set my flexible groups and flag misconceptions to confront in Lessons 1–2.

Measures readiness for: MS-LS1-1 (living things are made of cells) and the unit’s core vocabulary, before any of it is taught.

Part A · Quick Probes (circle one)

1. The smallest unit that is still considered “living” is the…

  • atom
  • cell
  • organ
  • molecule

2. Which of these is made of cells?

  • a tree
  • a fish
  • you
  • all of these

3. Agree or Disagree, then say why in one sentence: “Only animals are made of cells.”

I ___ (agree / disagree) because ____________________.

4. Agree or Disagree, then say why: “All cells come from other cells that already exist.”

I ___ (agree / disagree) because ____________________.

Part B · Draw & Label (no words needed to start)

In the box, draw what you think a cell looks like. Label any parts you already know the names of. If you’ve seen one under a microscope, draw what you remember.

[ drawing space ]

How I use the results

  • Misconception flags: Items 3–4 target the two most common 7th-grade misconceptions (plants/“non-animals” aren’t cellular; spontaneous generation). Disagreement patterns tell me what to confront directly in the cell-theory lesson.
  • Grouping: Part B drawings sort quickly into emerging / developing / secure prior schema, which seeds my Day 2–3 flexible groups.
  • No grade: Scored only as complete/incomplete so students answer honestly.

Differentiation

Multiple means of representation/expression (UDL)
The “draw a cell” task lets students who read below grade level — including my focus student Mateo — show prior knowledge without a reading bottleneck. Probes can be read aloud or run via text-to-speech.
English Learners (SIOP)
Key terms appear with a small picture glossary and Spanish cognates (cell / célula); oral or single-word responses are accepted on Parts A and C.
IEP/504 & ADHD
Short (15 min), low-stakes, chunked into three small parts; extended time and a quiet-start option available.
Gifted / secure-prior-knowledge
Stretch prompt: “Name one question scientists still can’t fully answer about cells.”

Authentic

“Design-a-Cell” Analogy Model & Presentation

When: Unit synthesis (Lesson 5), alongside the summative. Format: Performance task with an authentic audience. Use: Students apply the unit’s big idea to a real-world system of their choice and explain it — assessment as learning.

Measures: MS-LS1-2 & MS-LS1-3 (a cell as a system of interacting parts) through application, modeling, and oral/visual communication — the deepest evidence of transfer.

The Task

You are a “systems designer.” Choose a real system you know well — a city, factory, school, theme park, restaurant, or sports team — and build a model or labeled diagram that shows how that system is just like a cell. Then present it (live, or as a 2–3 minute recorded video) explaining:

  1. Which part of your system represents each major organelle, and why (match the job, not just the name).
  2. How the parts work together as a system — what happens to the whole if one part fails.
  3. One way your analogy is not perfect (where the comparison breaks down).

Audience: presented to the class (and shared with a 6th-grade class as “cell experts”).

Differentiation

Interest & choice (Tomlinson)
Students choose the system (sports, gaming, city, restaurant…), which raises engagement and lets cultural and personal background become an asset.
Multiple means of expression
Model can be physical, drawn, digital (slides), or a diorama; the explanation can be live or recorded — ideal for Mateo, who reasons strongly orally and draws excellent diagrams.
English Learners
Bilingual labels welcomed; the planning organizer and a rehearsal with a partner provide language support before presenting.
IEP/504 & ADHD
Organizer chunks the task; defined group roles; checkpoints across two days rather than one open-ended block.
Gifted
Extension: model a disease or malfunction (e.g., a mitochondria “power outage”) and trace the consequences up the levels of organization.
